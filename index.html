Motörhead

Albums About-Us

Motörhead were an English rock band formed in London in 1975 by bassist and lead vocalist Lemmy Kilmister, guitarist Larry Wallis and drummer Lucas Fox.Lemmy was also the primary songwriter and only constant member. The band are often considered a precursor to the new wave of British heavy metal, which re-energised heavy metal in the late 1970s and early 1980s.Though several guitarists and drummers have played in Motörhead, most of their best-selling albums and singles feature drummer Phil "Philthy Animal" Taylor and guitarist "Fast" Eddie Clarke.Motörhead released 23 studio albums, 10 live recordings, 12 compilation albums and five EPs over a career spanning 40 years. Usually a power trio, they had particular success in the early 1980s with several successful singles in the UK Top 40 chart. The albums Overkill, Bomber, Ace of Spades and, particularly, the live album No Sleep 'til Hammersmith cemented Motörhead's reputation as a top-tier rock band.The band are ranked number 26 on VH1's 100 Greatest Artists of Hard Rock.As of 2016, the band has sold more than 15 million albums worldwide.

Lemmy Kilmister

Ian Fraser Kilmister, known by his stage name Lemmy, was born on December 24, 1945, in Stoke-on-Trent, England. Renowned as the frontman of Motörhead, he stands as one of the iconic figures in the realm of rock and metal, leaving behind a legacy that defied musical boundaries. Lemmy's musical journey traces back to the late 1960s, making his initial mark with the psychedelic rock band Hawkwind. However, it was in 1975 that he took the pivotal step of forming Motörhead, a band that would redefine the landscape of heavy metal and punk with their fast-paced, high-energy sound. Lemmy's vocals, bass guitar skills, and lyrics played a pivotal role in shaping the unique identity of Motörhead. Recognizable by his iconic mustache, leather jackets, and Jack Daniel's whiskey, Lemmy became a symbol of rock'n'roll culture. His on-stage charisma and rugged demeanor solidified his status as a legendary figure. Motörhead's classic songs such as "Ace of Spades," "Overkill," and "Bomber" have left an indelible mark on the music industry. Lemmy's passing in 2015 marked a significant loss for the rock music community. However, his resilient spirit and music continue to be celebrated by countless fans. Lemmy Kilmister is remembered not only as a music icon but also as a legend whose lifestyle and rebellious spirit will endure through the ages.
